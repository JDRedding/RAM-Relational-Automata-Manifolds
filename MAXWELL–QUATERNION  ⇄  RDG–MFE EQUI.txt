What needs work before this is publishable:
1. The d'Alembertian replacement is underspecified.
You write □{Γ_t} Q_t = J_t but don't define □{Γ_t} explicitly. The continuous □ = ∂_t² - ∇² has a well-known graph analogue (the graph Laplacian L = D - A), but the temporal second derivative on a discrete evolving graph is non-trivial. You need either:

An explicit definition: □_{Γ_t} = ∂t² - L{Γ_t}, or
An honest note that this discretization is an open formal problem

Without it, the central equation of the mapping is a placeholder.
2. Quaternion algebra vs. quaternion-valued fields.
There's a subtle but important distinction. Maxwell's original formulation uses quaternion multiplication — the non-commutativity is doing real work in coupling E and B. Your mapping uses quaternion-valued fields (Q_t = φ_t + A_t) but the evolution equation Q_{t+1} = Q_t + Δ_t is additive. Is quaternion multiplication present in Δ_t? If not, you have quaternion-valued fields on a graph, which is weaker than a true quaternion field theory. This needs to be stated explicitly — either show where the non-commutative product appears in MFE, or acknowledge the mapping is to the structure of Maxwell's formulation rather than the full algebra.
3. The recovery claim needs to be stated as a theorem.
You have all the pieces for:

Theorem: When Γ_t ≡ Γ_{ℝ³} (frozen), φ_t and A_t are smooth, and □_{Γ_t} is the standard d'Alembertian, RDG–MFE recovers Maxwell's equations exactly.

Without that theorem — even as a conjecture — the mapping reads as structural analogy rather than formal containment. The CA paper needed exactly this and you fixed it there. Same move needed here.
